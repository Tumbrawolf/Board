# Board

A cooperative (with hidden-traitor potential), tactical resource-management board game for the tabletop, currently in active design. Players defend against waves of enemies across multiple combat lanes while managing a shared economy, recruiting and upgrading units, and completing missions — all while one or more players may secretly be working against the group.

> **Status:** Design in progress, with a full balance-and-rules playtest pass complete, plus 5 full manual playtest games (see `Playtest Game 1-5.md`) and 2 simulator-driven playtests (`Playtest Game 6-7.md`) validating the ruleset end to end. `Rules.docx` reflects every change below; a second, reader-friendly edition (`Board_Rules_Reorganized.docx`) restructures the same content for teaching new players and table-side lookup — both docs are kept in sync by hand, since there's no automatic link between them.

## Overview

Players act as commanders defending against escalating threats (fodder, infantry, drones, beasts, mechanised units, abominations, and tiered bosses) across active combat lanes. To survive, players:

- Recruit and rank up **units** (Infantry, Scout, Mech, Mech Scout, Vehicle) from Conscript through higher officer ranks.
- Spend a three-resource economy — **Organic, Tech, and Alien** — to recruit units, buy gear, and trigger location abilities.
- Staff shared **locations** (Barracks, Armory, Medical Bay, Containment Block, Command, Battlefield) to generate passive effects and unlock active abilities each round.
- Equip units with **Gear** (Armor, Weapon, Utility, Consumable), with restrictions for Infantry-only or Vehicle/Mech-only equipment.
- Play a **Tactician** — one of 17 commander roles (e.g. The Gunsmith, The Bulwark, The Drillmaster, The Quartermaster) — each granting unique passive and active abilities that shape strategy.
- Resolve random **Events** each round with pass/fail conditions and consequences.
- Complete **Missions** for escalating rewards, from early "kill 1 enemy" objectives to late-game resource/unit sacrifices.
- Pursue a **Secret Objective** aligned as Allied, Neutral, Saboteur, or Chaos — meaning not everyone at the table is rooting for the group's survival. Suspect someone? **Vote of No Confidence** (optional rule) lets the table formally accuse them, at real cost either way.
- Eventually face a **Boss** drawn from a pool of 15 named bosses, scaling across 5 tiers of difficulty.

## Playtesting

Physical board layouts for each location are provided as PNGs in `Boards/`. There's currently no Tabletop Simulator config or card art in the repo.

A Python balance simulator (not checked into this repo's CSV set, see the project's full file package) validated the core economy/combat numbers against thousands of simulated games, and individually audited every Command Card, Event, Tactician, Secret Objective, and Mission for whether its effect is genuinely modeled or needs game state that doesn't exist yet (documented per-card, nothing silently skipped). It hasn't yet been wired into a full running game loop, so combo/interaction testing between cards is still manual (table playtesting) rather than automated.

## Round Structure

Each round runs through four stages, plus actions players can take anytime:

1. **Planning Stage** — the commander spends from the **Command Pool** (a shared resource stockpile, separate from player resources, fed by donations and spent mostly on Location Upgrades) to activate command cards in hand; assigns each lane to a player (or covers it themself); draws 2 Events and picks one active; draws the round's Boss; and runs Mission Assignment draft-style (draw players + commander-rank mission cards, pass around the table one kept per pass until the pool is exhausted — a player can end up with 2+ new missions if it doesn't divide evenly). Then players place worker tokens at locations in turn order. **Sharing a location**: the first worker earns full income, every additional worker that round earns half (round down) — by default the lowest-Rank player there gets the full-income spot; at 4 players specifically, the first *two* workers both get full income (tested: 4-player tables need more breathing room than smaller ones for the same difficulty). Income is generated; players purchase Units/Gear in turn order, any number of times if they can afford it; shop slots (Barracks ×4, Armory ×2) refill instantly on purchase via the commander's choice of guaranteed Direct fill (Rank 1–3) or a Roll fill (1d8, scaled to the team's highest Rank); and players with a worker at Command may donate.
2. **Deployment Stage** — heal Med Bay units (Med Bay has 4 slots max, only 2 available until "Share Rooms" is bought; Containment Block has 2 slots max, both locked until "Containment Protocol" is bought); the commander assigns a Scout (the main counterplay to enemy Reveal effects); players rearrange units, retire them, or trade between lanes; equip gear. The commander then draws enemy cards based on current enemy progress and deals them into lanes.
3. **Combat Stage** — enemy reserves move to active, reveal effects trigger. The commander picks a starting lane; combat resolves lane by lane with leftover damage carried between fights; a lane clears or overruns. Bosses trigger their tier's ongoing effect (tiers advance by round count since drawn: T1 immediately, T2 at round 2, T3 at 4, T4 at 6, T5 at 8 — this is the final, settled rule; see Feedback #14), attack their targeted lane, and take damage from any lane with no enemies of its own.
4. **Cleanup Stage** — move lost units/gear to their piles; overrun lanes cost the player Progress Track 1 progress each; resolve the active Event; promote a player (rolling a d8 if 2 or 4 are tied for lowest Rank, d6 if 3 are tied); **Escalate**: skip entirely on Round 1, then every round after the enemy Progress Track advances by 1 and the player track advances by 1 if every lane survived — with player progress checked for the win *before* enemy progress advances each round, so a tie goes to the players.
5. **Anytime** — complete missions (promotes you at end of turn); use/equip gear; use unit abilities; retire a unit for a partial refund; activate command cards with a worker at Command; and, as an optional rule, call a **Vote of No Confidence** against a player you suspect is a Saboteur (or Chaos-aligned) — costly to call, genuinely rewarded if you're right and believed, costly if you're wrong.

## Feedback

A few things worth considering as the project matures:

1–13. *(See prior entries — repo cleanup, typo fixes, location-naming consistency, schema alignment, and git tracking. All resolved as of the last pass; not repeated here.)*

14. **Full ruleset playtest and balance pass completed.** Simulated thousands of games (2/3/4 players) against the core economy and combat numbers, hand-played several full games at the table, and fixed what surfaced:
    - **Escalate timing.** The original rule (enemy progress advances every round from Round 1) was mathematically close to unwinnable regardless of play skill. Fixed by skipping Escalate entirely on Round 1 only — every round after plays exactly as originally written. Tested at ~70% player win rate (4 players).
    - **Shop slots, fully specified.** The original rules only stated slot counts (Barracks ×4, Armory ×2) with no fill mechanic. Added: commander's choice per slot of a guaranteed Direct fill (Rank 1–3) or a gambled Roll fill (1d8, scales down to the team's highest Rank on overshoot — pure upside once the team's top Rank reaches 3+, a real risk below that).
    - **Worker-sharing rule, added.** Multiple workers can now share a location; the first earns full income, additional workers earn half. Several approaches were tested before landing here — a hard cap on workers-per-location worked but created unplayable cliffs between adjacent cap values; the smoother half-income version replaced it. Also found 4-player tables specifically need extra breathing room (the first *two* workers get full value there, not just one) to hit the same difficulty as 2–3 player tables.
    - **Boss Tier Escalation, specified and locked in.** The original data never stated what triggers a Boss's 5 tiers to advance. Settled on round-count-since-drawn (T1 immediate, then every 2 rounds) based on the pattern already present in every boss's tier text (T1 always "Passive is activated," T2/T4 always a flat stat boost, T3/T5 always a unique mechanic). This is the final rule, not provisional.
    - **Plate Host (Boss), Passive rewritten.** Its original Passive ("Enemies +1 Armor," all enemies, active from Round 1) could fully block all 3 Conscript-tier units from dealing any damage at all in the opening rounds — a hand-played game caught this. Two fixes were tried and rejected (delaying the whole passive left it with no identity at T1 unlike every other boss; a Conscript-only carve-out only mattered for a few rounds) before landing on: the Passive is now self-targeted ("Boss gains +1 Armor," not all enemies) for T1–T2, with the all-enemies version not kicking in until T3 — paired with a narrow rule that a lane fighting this specific boss at T1 can't be overrun, so a team that draws it early isn't handed an effective auto-loss.
    - **Vote of No Confidence, added (optional rule).** The base game had no way for the team to act on suspicion of a Saboteur — only a fully hidden card with zero interaction surface. Designed so the cost lands on the accuser specifically (escrowed resources/cards, returned + a promotion if right and believed; lost + a demotion if wrong), so it can't be used to grief the table. Not yet simulation-tested.
    - **2-player games run consistently easier** than 3–4 player across every mechanic tested (worker-sharing contention and shop-slot competition both naturally have less bite with fewer players). Decided against adding a dedicated fix — Difficulty Levels (Normal/Hard) already gives a 2-player table a lever to pull, and a documentation note now recommends using it.

15. **The Quartermaster, a new Tactician.** Designed to give the Roll-fill shop mechanic a dedicated build-around: Passive upgrades Roll-fill from 1d8 to 2d6 (capped at 8), Active rerolls one unwanted Roll-fill result per round. Added to `Tactician Cards.csv`, bringing the set to 18. (The Kingmaker was subsequently deleted, returning the count to 17.)

16. **Armor and Shields, promoted to real fields.** Previously buried as free-text inside ability descriptions on Units, Gear, and Enemies (e.g. "has 5 Armor and 15 Shields" sitting inside a sentence about something else entirely). Audited every CSV by hand to separate genuine flat base stats from conditional grants, auras to *other* units, and attack abilities that reference *enemy* armor — only true unconditional base stats were extracted. Result: 25 units, 9 gear items, and 9 enemies now have real `Armor`/`Shields` columns; `Rules.docx`'s card-breakdown sections updated to document the new fields.

17. **Keywords, audited and corrected.** A literal text-search pass had initially undercounted real usage — it found only 25 of the glossary's terms with an exact phrase match, when re-auditing by *meaning* (not just exact wording) confirmed every term in the glossary is genuinely used somewhere in the card pool, including high-frequency ones like **Targeted Damage** and **Penetration** that just hadn't been phrased the obvious way. Also found and consolidated two cases of the same full-sentence ability text repeated verbatim across many cards: **Long Range** ("Can Target any lane for damage when attacking," 5 units) and **Mobile** ("Can Move between Lanes when Reserve," 11 units) are now keywords instead of repeated prose. `Keywords.csv` is now verified at 38 entries.

18. **Several cards assume systems that aren't fully specified at the data/rules level yet.** Found while building a card-by-card balance simulator (which surfaces this kind of gap more reliably than reading alone, since every card has to resolve to *something*):
    - **Location Upgrades** — now defined (`Rules.docx`, Locations section): a location's "Upgrades" are its Command Cards with a Passive Effect; "fully upgraded" means owning all of them (count varies by location: Armory 3 up to Barracks 9 — not an arbitrary cap, just however many real upgrade cards exist).
    - **The Command Pool** — now defined as a shared stockpile separate from player resources, managed by the commander, fed by donations, spent mostly on Location Upgrades.
    - **Ability-activation as a trackable event** — now defined. A new **Ability Activation** Key Term (`Rules.docx`/`Board_Rules_Reorganized.docx`) specifies what counts: triggering a Primary/Secondary Ability, Active effect, or Instant effect (player choice or automatic trigger) on a Unit/Gear/Command Card/Tactician; Passive effects never count. Enemies have no separate Active field, so for them it's their Reveal effect triggering, or a triggered clause inside Passive text (on-hit/on-kill/on-death) — a flat always-on bonus in that same text doesn't count. Preventing an ability is tracked as a separate, distinct event.
    - **Mission Assignment fairness across player counts** — confirmed as a real issue, not just a theoretical one, and fixed as an optional rule (see #19 below).

None of these block playtesting as-is — the ruleset is in a genuinely playable, tested state. The open items above are refinements, not blockers.

19. **Tiered Mission Draw, added (optional rule).** Investigating #18's "untested assumption" turned up a real, quantifiable problem: under the default blind draw from one shuffled 117-card Mission deck, a 2-player game's Round 1 Mission Assignment draw (pool size 3) has roughly a **31.6% chance of drawing zero missions either starting player can complete** (both players are Conscript/Private, and only 37 of 117 missions are Rank 1–2) — versus 14.3% at 4 players (pool size 5). Smaller tables are disproportionately likely to get stuck holding only above-Rank missions early, with no reliable way to self-promote out of it (promotion otherwise depends on Event RNG or the commander spending on a Promotion Command Card). Added as an **optional rule** rather than the default, reusing the existing Shop Direct/Roll-fill pattern instead of inventing a new mechanic: split the Mission deck into Rank piles (like the Enemy Deck); Rank 1–3 missions are always the commander's free choice (no roll); Rank 4+ requires a 1d8 roll that scales down to (highest player Rank + 1) on overshoot. The default single-deck blind draw is unchanged and remains simpler for groups that don't mind an occasional dead draft.

20. **Vote of No Confidence, outcomes rebalanced.** Sanity-checking the math (flagged since #14 as "not yet simulation-tested") surfaced a real asymmetry: the rule had only one branch that ever confirmed the truth (a believed accusation), while a disbelieved one cost the accuser their entire escrow for zero information either way, and a believed-but-wrong accusation was punished by Rank loss alone with no effect on the accuser's own Secret Objective options. Rebalanced so a successful true accusation and a wrongly-believed false one now land symmetrically — both leave the exposed player (the caught traitor, or the wrongly-believing accuser) down to exactly 1 Secret Objective card, rather than one side getting a full reset and the other untouched: catching a real Saboteur/Chaos player now keeps any non-traitor card they're also holding instead of wiping both (a player isn't punished for genuinely also pursuing a good objective; if both their cards were traitor-aligned, they're dealt one new Allied objective revealed face-up to the table to confirm it's clean); a false accusation now also costs the accuser one of their own two cards, chosen and discarded by the player they wrongly accused (not the accuser themselves, so nobody can reliably shield their worse card). A rejected accusation (disbelieved either way) now fully refunds the accuser's escrow instead of confiscating it for nothing, with the accused player picking one accuser card to reveal (not discard) as the only consequence. Still not simulation-tested — treat as a second draft.

21. **`Location Actions.csv` cross-checked against `Rules.docx`'s Locations section and corrected.** This exported CSV had quietly drifted from the prose rules in three places that actually change numbers, not just wording: Containment Block's Alien income had drifted to "consume 1 enemy, gain its Rank" instead of the rules' "gain Alien = combined Rank of all contained enemies"; Battlefield's resource bonus had drifted to scaling **per commander Rank** instead of **per worker**; and Command Donations had picked up an unintended "skip your normal income to donate instead" trade-off that the rules never state (donating is free, on top of your normal benefit). All three corrected to match `Rules.docx`. Also fixed four cosmetic naming mismatches (Expansion Deck → Commander Deck, Active Expansions → Exhausted Commands, Player Lanes → Player Active, and split a merged "Recycling (Units and Gear)" row back into separate Recycling/Graveyard rows matching the rules' gear-only/units-only split). Along the way, found a genuine gap the CSV happened to already answer: `Rules.docx` never said where a dead enemy goes if Containment has no free Holding Cell — the CSV's existing **Enemy Disposal** pile (at Command) was the missing answer, now written into both rule docs.

22. **Card-breakdown field names brought in line with the actual CSV columns.** `Rules.docx`'s "Breakdown of card info" called Unit fields "Primary Ability"/"Secondary Ability" and the Units/Gear/Enemies "Health" field, but `Unit Stats.csv`/`Gear Stats.csv`/`Enemy Stats.csv` actually use `Main Effect`/`Bonus Effects` and `HP`. Renamed in both rule docs to match the data exactly — text-only fix, no rules or balance change.

23. **`Round Flow.csv` removed.** Checking it against the current round structure found it wasn't just "superseded" wording, it actively contradicted the current rules: its Escalate row had the *original*, already-fixed enemy-progress formula (no Round 1 grace period — the exact version Feedback #14 found "mathematically close to unwinnable"), and its Overrun row scaled progress loss by enemy Rank instead of the current flat -1 per overrun lane. Kept around, a future reader or a digital implementation could easily pull the wrong numbers from it instead of `Rules.docx`'s prose. Deleted rather than just re-labeled, since the actual current data lives in `Rules.docx`; full history is preserved in git if it's ever needed for reference (it did corroborate one thing — its "disposal pile" mention for dead enemies confirmed the **Enemy Disposal** location from #21 was part of the original design intent, not a guess).

24. **Unit/Enemy/Gear stats given real per-card variation instead of a flat Rank curve.** A full balance pass (data analysis, not playtesting) found Damage/HP/Cost were rigidly fixed by Rank across all 102 units and 93 enemies — every Captain-rank unit, for example, was exactly 5 Damage/5 HP/13 cost regardless of ability. Found one genuine duplicate-card bug in the process: `RDMP "Glass"` and `RDMP "Impailer"` (same Rank, same Damage/HP, *identical* ability text) had different costs — fixed to match. Built a 5-archetype model (Tank/Glass Cannon/Mobile/Support/Neutral) classified from each card's own Name + ability text, each with a fixed Damage/HP lean (e.g. Tank: -15% Damage/+15% HP) and a Rank-scaled Armor/Shields baseline-by-archetype-share (previously only 25/102 units and 9/93 enemies had any Armor/Shields at all; now 80/102 and 76/93 do — Glass Cannons stay at 0/0 by design). Cost scales with the resulting Damage+HP power shift only — Armor/Shields are treated as a free archetype trait, not a paid stat, to avoid runaway cost inflation. Extended to Scout Value (only the 20 genuine Scout-type units, leaned by archetype) and to `Gear Stats.csv` (same lean applied to each item's *existing* nonzero stats, preserving the Weapon=offense-only/Armor=defense-only discipline already in the data). Also found and fixed a parallel gap in Gear: only 1 of 66 items had a nonzero `Shields` stat despite several items' own Passive text explicitly granting Shields (`Shield Generator`, `Shield Projector`, and `Deployable Shield` — which was granting flat Armor instead of Shields despite its name) — same kind of "described in text, never extracted into the real stat column" issue as the original Armor/Shields fields fix (#16), just missed for Gear at the time.

25. **Gear's Equip/Activation cost model reworked.** The original rule charged a unit's Purchase Cost twice just to get a fresh item useful — once to buy it from the shop, once again to equip it onto a unit — with the same "pay again" applying every time gear moved to a different unit afterward. Reworked so equipping (or re-equipping elsewhere) is now always free, and the cost moved to *activating* an equipped item's Active effect (still its Purchase Cost, still limited to once per round); Passive effects remain always-on and free, and Consumables are exempt entirely (pay once at purchase, their single use is already included in that price). This also retired the old "equipped items can be equipped for free this round" Retire from Duty exception, since equipping is unconditionally free now, not just on retire-day. Ripple effect: 4 Event Cards (`Armor Supply Freeze`, `Production Fault`, `Weapons Allocation Freeze`, `Supply Chain Collapse`) explicitly manipulated the old equip cost ("Costs Tech = Rank to equip Armor") — reworded to reference the new activation cost instead, same numbers. The other 4 equip-related Events (`Armor/Utility/Weapons Recall`, `Total Disarmament`) didn't reference "equip cost" directly and needed no changes, though they're thematically a bit softer now since being forced to unequip no longer means paying again to re-equip later.

26. **Mission Cards' Export/Contribution "bundle" missions underpaid relative to their own single-resource siblings.** Found during the balance pass: `Full Export 1/3/5` and `Balanced Contribution I/II/III/IV/V` all required 3x the resources of their single-resource counterpart (e.g. donating 5 of *each* resource instead of 5 of one) but paid the same or worse reward per resource spent — at Tier 1, `Balanced Contribution I` gave the identical reward as donating just one resource type, despite costing 3x as much. Note this is specifically a *within-family* inconsistency, not a claim that Contribution and Export should pay the same as each other — Contribution intentionally pays less than Export at the same tier, since donated resources stay within the team's Command pool rather than being genuinely lost to supply. Fixed by matching each bundle mission's per-resource reward ratio to its own single-resource sibling at the same tier (8 missions touched; `Full Export 2/4/6` and `Balanced Contribution VI` already matched and were left alone). Also fixed an inconsistent base amount on `Full Export 5`/`Balanced Contribution V` (they required 25 of each resource while `Organic/Tech Export 5` required 30 at the same tier) — now 30 across the board.

27. **Win/loss conditions and enemy spawning fully redesigned.** Attempting a manual round-by-round walkthrough surfaced several rules that were either contradictory or never actually specified — not wording issues, but genuine holes in how a round resolves:
    - **Enemy draw formula was never specified.** "Draws enemy cards based on current enemy progress" had no actual formula for how many cards or what Rank. Now split into two independent axes: **Rank** comes from current Enemy Progress (even split across 0–10: Fodder 0-1, Grunt 2-3, Core 4-5, Advanced 6-7, Elite 8, General 9, Conqueror 10); **count per lane** comes from Difficulty × current Player Progress (Easy 2/3/3, Normal 3/4/5, Hard 4/6/7, stepping up at Player Progress 5 and 8) — deliberately *not* tied to Enemy Progress, so the "endless hoards" feeling scales with how close the team is to winning, not a flat clock.
    - **Boss draw cadence directly contradicted Boss Tier escalation.** Planning Stage drew "a Boss card for the round" every round, while Tier escalation assumed one Boss persisted across many rounds since being drawn — both can't be true. Resolved by making Boss spawning a dice-gated event (roll 1d10 only when no Boss is active; rolling at or below current Enemy Progress spawns one — impossible at Enemy Progress 0, guaranteed at 10) and making a Boss's Tier a live lookup from current Enemy Progress instead of a "rounds since drawn" timer (0-1=T1, 2-4=T2, 5-6=T3, 7-8=T4, 9-10=T5). A defeated Boss grants a one-round grace period before spawn rolls resume.
    - **Boss combat would have resolved like normal lane combat (fight to the death), which doesn't actually work for a multi-round Boss fight.** Any round a lane cleared its own enemies, "deal damage to the boss" under the normal Combat Cycle rule would either kill a 50 HP Boss outright or wipe that lane trying — Bosses would almost never survive long enough to reach later Tiers. Fixed: Boss combat is now one damage exchange per round (Boss hits its target lane once; each enemy-free lane hits the Boss once with its Active unit), which is what actually lets a Boss persist and escalate across several rounds.
    - **The loss condition itself was reworked.** Previously, Enemy Progress reaching 10 ended the game directly, racing against Player Progress reaching 10 — but escalating both enemy Rank *and* hoard count off the same number made the enemy track move too fast once any acceleration was added. Replaced with a third, dedicated **Overrun Tracker** (0–20, literally the two existing physical Progress Tracker components used end to end — no new component needed): starting position set by Difficulty (Easy 15, Normal 10, Hard 5), -1 per lane overrun each round, no healing, **0 loses the game**. Enemy Progress no longer ends the game by itself — it's purely a danger dial now (drives enemy Rank and Boss-spawn odds, caps at 10 and stays there). Overruns no longer cost Player Progress either; a clean round earns Player Progress same as before, an overrun round costs the Overrun Tracker instead, separating "missed opportunity to advance" from "took a real, permanent hit."
    - The old "tiebreak" note (player progress checked for a win before enemy progress advances, ties go to players) is removed — it only made sense when both tracks raced to the same kind of finish line, which is no longer the case.

28. **Redesign ripple effects: cards and rules that referenced the old loss condition, plus three more gaps found doing a "what's still assumed" pass.** Checking every card for "progress"/"overrun" references after #27 turned up real breakage, not just stale wording:
    - **`Trojan`/`Stubborn`/`Incompetence` (Secret Objectives) were unsatisfiable or trivial.** All three hinged on Player Progress *decreasing* ("lose 5 progress over the game," etc.) — but since overruns no longer cost Player Progress, nothing decreases it anymore. `Trojan` (Saboteur) could never trigger; `Stubborn` (Allied) was automatically always true. Redirected all three to the Overrun Tracker instead (e.g. Trojan: "Overrun Tracker drops by 5 or more over the course of the game"), preserving the same sabotage-vs-loyalty pairing.
    - **`Forward Command`** (Command Card) said "take half progress damage this round" — written for the old rule where overruns hit Player Progress. Now says "take half Overrun Tracker damage this round," the correct target under the new model.
    - **`Deus Machina`** (Chaos Secret Objective) said "when the team would lose the game, instead set progress to what it was before this round" — now explicit that the trigger is the Overrun Tracker reaching 0, and the rollback restores the Overrun Tracker specifically, not an unspecified "progress."
    - **Scout Value's mechanical meaning was never actually defined** ("Value when used as a scout" didn't say what it did, and the existing prose claimed it controlled *how many* enemies get scouted, which doesn't match the Organic/Tech/Alien 3-way split at all). Redefined: Scout Value is now passive income, added to the Command pool the moment a unit is assigned as that round's scout. *How many* enemies get revealed is now its own, separate, simple rule — 1 by default, with several units' own ability text already granting more (`Civilian Survivalist`'s "Scouts +1 enemy," `"Python"`'s "Reveals 2x units when scouting") layering on top of that baseline.
    - **Default combat turn order was never stated.** Several unit abilities say "Attacks 1st," implying normal combat has no inherent first-actor — but nothing said who actually goes first by default. Now explicit: the enemy's Active card deals damage first each exchange by default, the player's Active unit responds, unless an ability grants the player's unit priority instead.
    - **Scout pool empty edge case** — if no Scout-type unit has ever been donated, Assign Scouts is now explicitly skipped that round rather than left ambiguous.
    - **A follow-up sweep of every CSV (not just the ones already checked) for "progress"/"overrun" turned up two more broken enemy cards and a stale component description.** `Tunnler`'s Passive ("reduce progress by 1") and `Tactician`'s Reveal/Passive ("Reduce the progress by 3" / "Lose 2 progress on death") both described reducing Player Progress directly, which is no longer possible — redirected both to the Overrun Tracker, the same fix as the Secret Objectives. `Location Actions.csv`'s Progress Track description ("lost through damage to base") was also still describing the old single-track model — updated to make clear Player Progress is gain-only now and the Overrun Tracker is what actually takes damage. (`Siege Ender`'s "10 damage per progress" was left alone — it's a scaling reference, not an action, and works fine pointing at either Progress Track, so it's ambiguous but not actually broken.)

29. **Four new cards added to give players direct counterplay against Enemy Progress and the Overrun Tracker** (previously the only levers on either were a couple of Mission rewards). All deliberately capped to avoid letting any one card indefinitely suppress the difficulty curve ("stalling"):
    - **`Recon Satellite`** (Gear, Utility, Major): "This effect can trigger at most 3 times per game: whenever you complete a Mission, Reduce Enemy Progress by 1." A flat per-game cap rather than a per-round one — an earlier draft capped the *cadence* instead (once per round), which doesn't actually bound the total suppression over a long game.
    - **`Saboteur Cell`** (Unit, Infantry Scout, Major): "This effect can trigger at most 3 times per game: when used as the active scout, Reduce Enemy Progress by 1." Same per-game cap, for the same reason.
    - **`Strategic Withdrawal`** (Command Card, Command): "Once per game: Reduce Enemy Progress by 3, but lose 1 Player Progress this round." A deliberate Player Progress cost on a card *effect* — distinct from the now-removed automatic overrun-costs-Player-Progress rule; effects can still cost Player Progress, only the passive overrun mechanic can't.
    - **`Last Stand Beacon`** (Gear, Consumable, Specialist): "Usable only while Enemy Progress is 8 or higher: Sacrifice 2 Player Progress, restore 5 Overrun Tracker." The first and only way to restore the Overrun Tracker at all — deliberately rare (Consumable, single use), expensive (costs the team's own win-condition progress), and gated to the late-game danger window so it can't be used as routine maintenance.

30. **Location Upgrade slots, corrected back to their original intent.** Previous documentation (`Rules.docx`'s Location Upgrades paragraph) described a location's slot count as "simply however many real upgrade cards exist for that building, not a separate invented cap" — but `Architect` ("Fully upgrade 3 buildings") and `Minimalist` ("End game with 3 empty upgrade slots") were both written assuming a genuinely limited slot count, smaller than the full card pool, that a team has to make real choices about. That original intent had been lost somewhere along the way. Restored: locations now have a real Upgrade slot cap — Armory 1, Medical Bay 2, Command 2, Battlefield 3, Containment Block 3, Barracks 4 — distinct from the larger pool of available Passive-bearing cards a team can choose from (Armory 3, Medical Bay 4, Command 5, Battlefield 6, Containment Block 7, Barracks 9). A location is "fully upgraded" once its (smaller) slot count is filled, not once every card for that building has been bought. Once at capacity, a player may swap an active upgrade for a different one: deactivating the old one is free, but it's discarded for good (never rebuildable at that location again), and the replacement costs its full price as normal. `Architect`/`Minimalist` needed zero wording changes — they were already correct; only the surrounding rules documentation had drifted. Added the equivalent explanatory paragraph to `Board_Rules_Reorganized.docx`'s Section 4 too, which never had one at all (a separate, pre-existing gap between the two docs).

31. **Fodder-rank enemies lose their special-effect text.** `Pests` and `Ticks` (the entire Fodder rank — Enemy Progress 0-1, the game's gentlest tier) had their `Reveal`/`Passive` columns blanked in `Enemy Stats.csv`; they're now plain stat-stick filler (Damage/HP only, no ability text). The 3-game manual playtest found Pests' "+1 damage for each other revealed Pest" Passive produced genuinely swingy results right at the table's first taste of combat (a 1-damage Fodder card hitting for 6 once enough copies stacked), and the same tier raised an unresolved board-wide-vs-lane-local scoping question (Playtest Log #34, Game 1) that's simplest to retire entirely rather than rule on, given Fodder's job is to be the gentle intro tier, not a source of swingy edge cases. Added an explicit note to `Rules.docx`/`Board_Rules_Reorganized.docx`'s Enemies card-breakdown explaining this is by design.

32. **Round 0 added: a prep round before Round 1, with no enemies and no Combat Stage.** Both rule docs' Turn Stages / Round Structure section now describe Round 0 — full Planning and Deployment Stages (worker placement, income, purchasing, Command Donations, healing, scouting, reassigning, equipping) run exactly as normal, but no enemies are drawn into any lane and there is no Combat Stage at all. Round 0's Cleanup only does Managing the Dead (trivially empty) and Command Card Refill; it skips Overrunning Lanes, Event Resolution, Promotions, and Escalate entirely, since none of those have anything to act on yet. This directly targets the "early-game is too resource-starved" root cause the 3-game playtest batch kept circling — see #34/#36 below for the other prongs of that same fix. Ripple effect on the commander-holdover rule (Playtest Log #31): since Round 0 is now the round with no previous round, the Leader-fills-in-as-commander exception moves from Round 1 to Round 0; Round 1 properly holds its commander over from Round 0's own Worker Placement winner, the same as every later round holds over from the one before it. The Initial Setup / "1st turn actions" flow in both docs now says Round 0 follows Leader Selection, before Round 1 begins.

33. **Combat clarifications batch, all confirmed in the 3-game playtest review (Playtest Log #34) and now written into both rule docs explicitly:**
    - **Post-combat Lane Reinforcement** (new Combat Stage subsection, after Boss events, before Cleanup): once every lane has resolved its normal Combat Cycle for the round, a lane that fully cleared its enemies may move some or all of its surviving units into a different lane that still has enemies, fighting one additional exchange there under normal Combat Cycle rules. This can rescue a lane from an overrun, since it resolves before the Overrunning Lanes check, and works regardless of which lane order resolved first.
    - **Reserve promotion happens the same round, not next round.** When a lane's Active unit or enemy dies mid-round, the next Reserve on that side immediately becomes Active and keeps fighting that same round (Combat Cycle) — "move to next lane" only happens once a side has no more Reserve to promote at all, not on every individual death. This directly overturns the opposite reading three separate games independently used during the playtest batch (Game 2), so it's now spelled out rather than left ambiguous.
    - **Gear survives a unit's combat death, but only 1 item.** Previously gear was ruled lost alongside its unit on any combat death (Playtest Log #34, Game 1), with only a deliberate Retire from Duty saving it. Now the unit's owner picks exactly 1 equipped item to keep (returns to hand, like a Retire) when the unit dies in combat; anything beyond that 1 item is lost for good — a middle ground between "lose everything" and "lose nothing."
    - **Enemy lane-movement effects now explicitly prioritize a lane that still has a living player unit** over a lane that's already fully wiped, generalizing the ad-hoc ruling the playtest needed for Lance Turret's "swap to the lane with the lowest-damage active unit" (Playtest Log #34, Game 3) to any similar repositioning effect.
    - **Harmful enemy effects target the player side, beneficial enemy effects target enemies (including itself), by default.** This was the working assumption used throughout all 3 playtest games; now written as an explicit default-targeting rule next to the Enemies card-breakdown's Reveal/Passive fields, so individual cards only need to call out an exception when their text actually differs from this default.
    - **Enemy Scouting's draw-and-reveal sequencing, spelled out step by step:** draw the round's full enemy total into one shared pool first, apply scout reveals against that whole pool (not per-lane), reshuffle the full pool (face-up and face-down together), then deal into lanes. This guarantees a scouted card lands in a random lane instead of being pre-assigned before the reveal happens — the previous text was ambiguous about which of these steps came first.

34. **Cleanup/edge-case clarifications batch, all confirmed in the 3-game playtest review and written into Key Terms and the relevant stage sections of both rule docs:**
    - A lane with enemies present and zero player units — never deployed, not just dead — counts as Overrun exactly like a lane whose defender died (this was reached independently by two of the three playtest games, see Playtest Log #34).
    - The Overrun Tracker hitting 0 ends the game **immediately**; no remaining Cleanup sub-steps (Event Resolution, Promotions, Escalate) run once it happens, even mid-Cleanup.
    - "At combat start" (as referenced by Event Failure Penalties) means the moment that round's Combat Stage begins — since Failure Penalties are only checked at Cleanup, after that round's combat already happened, any such penalty necessarily applies to the *next* round's combat, never retroactively.
    - Promotions' "the commander may select another player to promote" now explicitly reads "(never themselves)."
    - If a drawn Event's Completion Condition depends on game state that isn't currently possible (e.g. requiring 2 Active Vehicles before any Vehicle Bay unlock exists), it's discarded and replaced before the 2 choices are presented to the commander, rather than potentially handing the table a dead card.

35. **Command Donations no longer require a worker placed at Command, for either resources or donating a Scout-eligible unit.** Previously gated behind having a worker at Command that round; now any player can donate resources to the Command pool, or donate a Scout-eligible unit for use as a Scout, at any time during Planning. This removes a second friction point on the same early-game resource bottleneck #32/#36 also target, and stops a team's only scout source from being blocked by an unrelated worker-placement squeeze.

36. **Two small economy levers added, both flagged as designer recommendations, not yet battle-tested — revisit after the next playtest:** each player now starts the game with 2 Organic in addition to their existing starting cards (Player Setup); and completing a Mission now grants an *additional* +1 Rank (so +2 total) if the mission's bracketed Rank is at least 3 tiers above the completing player's current Rank at the time of completion, capped at +2 total per mission (previously a flat +1 regardless of stretch). Both are aimed at the same "early game is too resource/Rank-starved" signal the 3-game playtest batch kept surfacing, alongside Round 0 (#32) and the Command Donations change (#35) — deliberately not stacking on a 4th lever this pass so the existing fixes can be evaluated on their own first.

37. **Empty scout pool no longer disables scouting entirely — it just loses the income.** Previously, no Scout-type unit donated meant skipping the Assign Scouts step entirely (no enemy reveal at all that round). Now the commander still gets the normal baseline scouting effect (choose 1 enemy card to reveal) even with an empty scout pile; only the Scout Value income is lost, since there's no scout unit generating it. This stops an empty pool from silently disabling the team's only counterplay to enemy Reveal effects on top of losing the income.

38. **Tech cost to equip Gear, reintroduced and scaled to the gear's Rank — a deliberate partial reversal of #25.** Equipping gear onto a unit (including re-equipping it elsewhere) now costs Tech equal to the gear's Rank number (Conscript=1 ... Brigadier=8), paid each time it's equipped, on top of the existing Activation cost from #25. The 3-game playtest batch found players consistently sitting on a Tech surplus with nothing worth spending it on; #25 had moved *all* equip-related cost to Activation specifically to stop double-charging a unit's Purchase Cost, but that left equipping itself completely free, which turned out to remove too much of Tech's only sink. This doesn't undo #25's fix (Activation still costs Purchase Cost again, still once per round) — it adds back a second, smaller, Rank-scaled cost specifically at the equip step.

39. **Decks formalized as never-exhaustible, and the Unit/Gear decks split into Rank-based piles like the Enemy deck already was.** Two related fixes to `Managing Decks` (Initial Setup) and `Purchasing new cards` (Planning Stage): first, the Unit deck (post Vehicle/Mech removal) and Gear deck (post Experimental removal) are now separated into per-Rank piles at setup, the same way the Enemy deck already was — so Direct fill (Rank 1-3, commander's choice) draws straight from the relevant Rank pile instead of searching through one shuffled deck and skipping past higher-Rank cards. Second, this is generalized into an explicit standing rule: *any* card drawn, examined, or passed over without being kept by anyone — unsold shop cards at Cleanup, over-Rank cards skipped during a Direct fill search, anything similar — always returns to the bottom of its deck/pile rather than being removed from the game; decks are never treated as exhaustible. PLAYTEST CHANGE: this directly resolves a tooling gap the 3-game playtest batch ran into repeatedly (Playtest Log #34) — both the Unit deck and, earlier, the Enemy Fodder pile (#33 in the Playtest Log) ran dry mid-game purely because the playtest's draw tooling had no skip-and-return mechanism; the rules now say explicitly what a real physical deck already does naturally.

40. **Command Card hand size raised to 3 (non-commander) / 4 (commander), and the stale Planning-stage refill text finally replaced with the already-decided Cleanup-stage version.** `Rules.docx` had literally read "must draw until they hold 2 cards... 3 for commander" at the top of Planning, even though Playtest Log #32 had already established (in a prior session) that refill actually happens at Cleanup, once that round's commander is finalized — the docx text was just never updated to match. Both fixed in the same pass: hand sizes raised 2→3 / 3→4, and the refill rule itself moved into a new "Command Card Refill" subsection at the end of Cleanup Stage in both rule docs, with the stale Planning-stage sentence now pointing there instead of restating an old number.

41. **Rank Trickle added: every player gains +1 Rank automatically every 2 rounds.** A single-game playtest of the #31-40 revision (Playtest Game 4, 2026-06-25) confirmed the early-resource-starvation fixes worked — 3 clean rounds with zero Overrun Tracker damage, versus all 3 previous games already bleeding by then — but the team still lost outright at Round 6, once Grunt/Core-rank enemies arrived. Root cause: unit deploy-Rank is gated to a player's own Rank, and Rank only climbs via Mission completion or commander Promotion (no self-promotion) — both unreliable — while Enemy Progress climbs a guaranteed, unconditional +1/round. 2 of 4 players in that game never left Conscript Rank the entire game, capped to 1 Damage/1 HP units against enemies with 4-12 Damage/4-15 HP, despite the team's Command pool sitting on 29/26/12 resources nearly idle at the moment of loss — resources were never the constraint, Rank was. Fix: a new **Rank Trickle** rule (Cleanup Stage, both rule docs) — every player automatically gains +1 Rank every 2 rounds (end of Round 2, 4, 6...; Round 0 doesn't count toward this), capped at Brigadier, stacking with whatever Missions/Promotions already grant. This gives Rank the same kind of guaranteed floor Enemy Progress already has, rather than leaving it entirely dependent on RNG/commander discretion. **Designer addition, not yet battle-tested** — flagged for confirmation after the next playtest, same as #36's two economy levers.

42. **Three-pronged fix for board-wide enemy Reveal damage, after Rank Trickle (#41) fixed the Rank-floor problem but didn't stop the loss.** A follow-up playtest (Playtest Game 5, 2026-06-25) confirmed Rank Trickle worked exactly as designed — final Rank spread Sergeant/Sergeant/Captain/Major, versus Game 4's Private/Conscript/Conscript/Sergeant at the same checkpoint — but the team still lost at the identical round (Round 6) via the identical mechanism. Both games' Round 4 saw all 4 lanes wiped simultaneously by a single enemy Reveal effect that damages every lane at once ("2x damage to all lanes" or similar) — a spike that punishes the whole board regardless of how tough any individual unit has gotten, which per-unit Rank/stat improvements don't directly counter. Checking the playtest record: both teams fielded exactly 1 scout the entire game (donated early, never replaced or upgraded even after Game 4's scout died mid-game), revealing only 2 of each round's 12 dealt enemies — 10 of 12 enemies went completely unscouted every round, including whichever one carried the board-wide Reveal that ended the game. Three changes, deliberately stacked rather than picked one-at-a-time (easier to dial back individually later if any one proves overtuned than to under-fix and need a 2nd follow-up pass):
    - **Default scouting reveal count raised from 1 to 2 enemies per round** (Assign Scouts, Deployment Stage) — directly increases how much of a round's hoard gets pre-scouted before Reveal effects can fire as a surprise, still stacking with unit-text bonuses like Civilian Survivalist's own "Scouts +1 enemy."
    - **Multi-lane damage cap**: an enemy Reveal/Passive effect that damages more than one lane at once now deals full damage only to the enemy's own lane; every other lane it reaches takes half that damage, rounded down (minimum 1, same floor as Armor) — added next to the existing Default Targeting Rule (Enemies card-breakdown, both rule docs).
    - **New Command Card, "Early Warning Network"** (Battlefield, Cost O5/T5/A5, `Command Cards.csv` 61→62): Passive (build) reduces all-lane Reveal/Passive damage by a further flat 2 per lane; Active (instant) halves it for the round instead, for a team that needs the help right now rather than banked for later. Gives players a real lever to invest in, rather than only a passive global nerf.
    
    All three are **designer additions, not yet battle-tested** — watch for over-correction (a board that's now too safe from Reveal effects) as much as under-correction when the next playtest runs.

43. **Event card audit and redesign pass (2026-06-28): two cards deleted, three cards redesigned and fully wired into the web-app engine.** All 40 Event cards reviewed end-to-end against the TypeScript engine; round effects, conditions, rewards, and penalties cross-checked and implemented. Two cards were cut entirely; three required redesign rather than just wiring:
    - **Forced Re-Armament** (Equipment costs doubled) and **System Lockdown** (Return all upgrades to hand) deleted. Both cards' effects either duplicated existing mechanics or had no clean implementation path in the current engine; removed from `Event cards.csv` and all engine branches, bringing the Event card total to 38.
    - **Ion Storm** redesigned. Old round effect ("move friendly shields to enemies") replaced with "all enemies gain +5 shields; all shield damage dealt is doubled this round" — the original wording was inconsistent and the new mechanic is both cleaner and more interesting. Condition raised to 40 shields destroyed in a single round (from the old 25 — the round effect alone guarantees ~20 shields' worth of absorption, making 25 trivially achievable). Reward is now a permanent standing effect: scouted units enter combat with 0 shields. Penalty is also permanent: enemies permanently enter each combat with +10 shields. Shield absorption is tracked per-round via a new `shieldsDestroyedThisRound` counter accumulated from both call sites to `resolveLaneCombat`.
    - **Renovations** redesigned. Old round effect ("block new builds at upgraded locations") replaced with "set aside all currently built upgrades for the duration of the round; new builds proceed normally; upgrades are restored after event resolution, with any overflow (if slots filled during the round) returning to the commander's hand." This gives the event a more interesting texture — a temporary disruption followed by a restoration puzzle — rather than a hard block. Reward unlocks the ability to remove an already-built upgrade when building at a full location (cheapest regular upgrade displaced, returned to deck). Penalty strips all built upgrades at end of every round while it persists.
    - **Annihilation Clause** wired. Round effect now correctly applies to all combatants (both sides): every unit is deleted on death, bypassing saves (`cannotDie`, Chronostasis, Revive Once, Doctor save all suppressed) and containment. Condition corrected from 2x kills-to-deaths to a straight majority (kills > deaths this round). Reward: permanent — enemies are deleted (saves suppressed) when killed by a player whose Rank exceeds the current enemy tier's numeric rank. Penalty: permanent — player units skip all saves when killed by an enemy tier whose numeric rank exceeds the killing player's Rank.
